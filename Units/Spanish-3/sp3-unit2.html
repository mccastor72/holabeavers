Unit 02

El condicional

Conditional & Hypotheticals

📅 Weeks 4–6

Learning Objectives

  • Form the conditional tense with regular and irregular verbs
  • Use the conditional to express what would happen
  • Combine conditional with imperfect subjunctive in si clauses
  • Express polite requests and suggestions with the conditional
  • Discuss hypothetical scenarios in writing and conversation

Grammar Explanations

Conditional Tense Formation
Add endings to the full infinitive: -ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían. Example: hablar → hablaría, hablarías... Same endings for all verb types. Note: same irregular stems as simple future.
Irregular Conditional Stems
Same stems as future irregulars: tener→tendr-, poder→podr-, querer→querr-, venir→vendr-, salir→saldr-, hacer→har-, decir→dir-, saber→sabr-. Add conditional endings to these stems.
Polite Requests with Conditional
The conditional makes requests more polite: ¿Podría hablar más despacio? (Could you speak more slowly?) Me gustaría una mesa para dos. (I would like a table for two.) Much softer than using the present tense.
Conditional in Si Clauses
Past hypotheticals: Si + pluperfect subjunctive, + conditional perfect: Si hubiera estudiado, habría pasado el examen. Present hypotheticals: Si + imperfect subj., + conditional: Si tuviera tiempo, viajaría más.
