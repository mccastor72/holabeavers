Cuestiones globales

Global Issues & Current Events

Learning Objectives

  • Discuss global issues such as poverty, climate, and human rights
  • Read and summarize authentic news articles in Spanish
  • Use passive voice and impersonal expressions
  • Express opinions and debate using advanced discourse markers

Grammar Explanations

Passive Voice: Ser + Participle
Form passive voice with ser + past participle (agrees in gender/number with subject): El acuerdo fue firmado por los líderes. (The agreement was signed by the leaders.) Often used in news writing.
Impersonal Se
Use se + third person for general statements without specifying who: Se necesitan más recursos. (More resources are needed.) Se dice que la situación mejorará.
Persuasive Writing Connectors
Strengthen arguments with: Es evidente que (it is evident that), No cabe duda de que (there is no doubt that), Cabe destacar que (it is worth noting that), Hay que reconocer que (one must recognize that).
Subjunctive for Doubt/Opinion
Use subjunctive after expressions of doubt: No es cierto que haya una solución fácil. Use indicative after expressions of certainty: Es cierto que el problema existe.
